> objection lands on scar tissue before capital is in the room. Four hostile
> professionals attacked the BUSINESS (the app has been beaten separately):
> a procurement/vendor-risk director, a national-rental competitor's sales rep,

## 2. The spoken layer — answers delivered BEFORE the question forms

**The origin line (every intro, unprompted):**
> "I ran temporary power for over a decade — I built Sunbelt's EMaaS program.
> The app is what the job taught me, written down so it does math."

**The independence line (kills competitor talk tracks #1–5 preemptively):**
> "We don't compete with the rental companies — we're the independent second
> opinion that keeps their quotes honest. Every kW they spec, they get paid on.
> We don't sell iron, so our number is just the truth. Run our report, hand it
> to three rental houses, and watch the quotes drop. The disclaimer on our PDF
> isn't weakness — it's the difference between an advisor and a salesman.
> Ask the rep whether his sizing tool disclaims anything, or just rounds up."

**The 2 AM answer:**
> "You're right — we don't roll trucks, and you should absolutely have a
> national provider under contract for that. We're the reason their truck is
> the right size before it rolls."

**The two-lines answer (Ruth's unifying sentence, sequenced not juggled):**
> "We help operators of critical infrastructure answer one question — will the
> power stay on when it matters — first with free engineering tools that prove
> we know the math, then with paid advisory when the answer is no. Security
> consulting is the current revenue engine; the app builds the audience."

**The procurement call (60 seconds, concedes everything first):**
> "I'm not asking to engineer your job — your EOR does that. I'm asking for a
> capped advisory scope where I've spent twelve years. My sizing goes to your
> PE as a checked input, never a stamp. E&O binds this week, the vendor package
> is on your desk Friday. Cap my scope, hold back 10%, judge me on the first
> deliverable."

**The investor paragraph (the honest version that earns meeting two):**
> "I spent twelve years watching companies overspend on temporary power because
> nobody in the room could do the math independently. I built the math and gave
> it away — it's my credential and my funnel. Here's the pipeline, here's what's
> signed, here's what's hope — and I'll tell you which is which."

## 3. Decisions only Erik can make (the open business items)

1. **Bind E&O + GL** (~$1.5–4k/yr; Hiscox / BiBerk / Vouch / Embroker).
   Unblocks procurement kill #1 AND is the attorney's #4. The single highest-ROI
   dollar in this document. "Going bare is the most expensive money you'll ever save."
2. **Terms of Use** — one lawyer engagement (~$2–5k): clickwrap arbitration +
   class waiver + liability cap + GA venue + express user duty to verify.
   Attorney's verdict: do this + the copy fixes + the RH fence and SG is
   "not worth naming in the suit."
3. **The funnel decision** — gate PDF export behind an email address, or not.
   Ruth ranked monetization: (1) lead magnet for advisory retainers — do now;
   (2) white-label to rental companies (Herc/United/Aggreko — Erik knows these
   buyers) — one deal is a business; (3) paid pro tier — later; (4) per-report
   pricing — no; (5) vendor sponsorship — never, it kills the neutrality moat.
4. **PE teaming agreement** — a named licensed engineer who reviews on retainer.
   Converts "the app disclaims itself" from weakness to workflow.
5. **Vendor package weekend** — W-9, COI (once bound), banking verification,
   NDA template, one-page data-handling statement, scope-letter template
   ("advisory input to your engineer of record, never engineer of record").
   Procurement's minimum ticket for a sub-$50k engagement; a weekend of paperwork.
6. **Optional: 1-hr IP attorney** read of the SBR release (questions pre-written
   in the Provenance file) — defuses the trademark grenade before anyone throws it.

## 5. The doctrine

An objection answered before it's asked reads as identity; answered after, it
reads as defense. Everything in §2 gets said in the first sixty seconds, in the
deck, in the app's own copy — so the room never contains the skeptic. It just
contains people who already know.
